In 79 AD, Mount Vesuvius erupted, destroying much of the city with volcanic debris and covering it in 19 feet of ash. For centuries the ruins of Pompeii lay undisturbed and perfectly preserved by the thick blanket of ash. In the 1700s, the discovery of the once-hidden city was revealed to the world.
