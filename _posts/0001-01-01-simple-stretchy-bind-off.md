{% raw %}So simple, and so stretchy!  This is my new favorite bind-off, good to use whether you need a little or a lot of stretch.  A good matching cast-on is the German Twisted CO, video here:  <br /><a rel="nofollow" target="blank" href="http://youtu.be/UcwmCuIylII">http://youtu.be/UcwmCuIylII</a><br /><br />Instructions for working this BO in 2x2 rib:<br />Step 1:  K 2 stitches, insert the left needle into the front of the two stitches you just worked, wrap the back needle and knit them together (k2tog tbl)<br />Step 2:  P 1 stitch, Insert left needle into the back of the two stitches you just worked, wrap the front needle and purl them together (p2tog)<br />Step 3:  Repeat Step 2<br />Step 4:  K 1 stitch, insert the left needle into the front of the two stitches you just worked, wrap the back needle and knit them together (k2tog tbl)<br />Step 5:  Repeat Step 4<br />Repeat Steps 2 through 5<br /><br />Instructions for working this stitch in any other stitch combination:<br />K every knit stitch, and k2tog tbl it with the other stitch on the right needle<br />P every purl stitch, and p2tog it with
