Die with Zero By Bill Perkins Summary - Optimise your life and your spending to get what you want out of life. I share Bill Perkin’s incredible wisdom and philosophies about life with regard to optimising your time and your money throughout your life.

DIE WITH WITH ZERO: https://amzn.to/3CfizpY

In this video I give a chapter by chapter summary and my top takeaways from the book. This book really challenged my thinking and has changed the way I think about our finances now.

Chapter 1: Invest in Experiences
- Life is the sum of your experiences so it’s important to invest in experiences so that When you look back on your life, the richness of those experiences will determine your judgement of how full a life you’ve led.

Chapter 2: Why Die With Zero
- If you spend hours and hours of your life acquiring money and then die without spending all of that money, then you’ve needlessly wasted too many precious hours of your life. There is just no way to get those hours back.

Chapter 3: How to Spend Your Money
- You should be focusing on maximising your life enjoyment rather than on maximising your wealth.

Chapter 4: What about the Kids?
- You don’t need to wait to give money to your children and the charities that you care about because as the book explains a person’s ability to extract enjoyment out of money declines with age.

Chapter 5: Balance Your Life
- This is all about finding the optimal balance between enjoying the present and providing for a good future. This includes investing in your health, because people of all ages should be spending more time and money on their health’ because ‘earlier investments in health would actually yield greater lifetime fulfilment’.

Chapter 6: Start to Time-Bucket Your Life
- Time bucketing your life is all about thinking about what experiences you want to have at certain ages. This makes a plan so make sure that you experiences those events at the age when they will be of most benefit.

Chapter 7: Know Your Peak
- Most people’s net worth continues to grow into their 70’s and Perkins wants us to challenge this idea because that money isn’t as much use to us at that age.

Chapter 8: Be Bold Not Foolish
- Be bold not foolish means taking risks at the right time in your life and most of the time that will be when you’re young with fewer commitments.
